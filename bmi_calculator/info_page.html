What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple calculation using your height and weight to estimate if you're underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]²

Note: BMI does not differentiate between fat and muscle mass — athletes or muscular people may show a high BMI without having excess fat.

What is BFP (Body Fat Percentage)?

BFP (Body Fat Percentage) is the percentage of your body weight that is fat. It provides a more detailed view of your health compared to BMI, as it distinguishes between fat mass and lean mass (muscles, bones, water, etc.).

Why BFP is useful:

  • BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight.
  • BFP reflects fitness and fat composition more accurately.
  • Useful for tracking fitness progress and overall health.
